How to Use This Calculator
A complete guide for new recruiters. Read this once and you will understand exactly how nurse staffing economics work and how to use every part of this tool.
Layout Overview
The calculator uses a two-column layout. The left panel holds all your inputs — bill rate, hours, W2, stipend, toggles. The right panel shows results that update instantly as you type. There are no "Calculate" buttons — everything is live.
💰
Left Panel — Inputs
Billing section first (bill rate, MSP fee, hours, weeks, client picker), then Nurse Package (W-2, stipend, benefits, toggles). Scroll to see all fields.
📊
Right Panel — Results
Hourly profit margin (green or red), project billing total, blended rate, hourly breakdown, and weekly breakdown. Live-updates as you change inputs.
🧮
Top Tabs — Mode
Switch between Standard, California OT, and OGS Nurse calculation modes. Each has different formulas and field requirements.
🕒
History (Auto-Saved)
Every calculation is automatically saved for 7 days. Click the History tab to review past packages and reload any one back into the calculator.
The Foundation — Bill Rate Driven Accounts
Every deal starts with one number: the bill rate. This is the hourly amount a hospital pays HonorVet for placing a nurse. From that single number, you decide how much to pay the nurse and what's left as profit. The math runs in this order:
StepCalculationExample
Bill rateWhat the hospital pays$85.00/hr
MSP fee takenStaffing marketplace cut (3-7%)−$5.53 (6.5%)
Net bill rateWhat HonorVet keeps after MSP$79.48/hr
Pay the nurseW2 + overhead + stipend−$61.41/hr
Profit marginWhat remains$18.06/hr
Why W2 + Per Diem? (The Core Concept)
There are two ways to pay a nurse, and the difference matters enormously. W2 Only means the entire compensation is taxable wages. W2 + Per Diem means a smaller W2 wage plus a non-taxable stipend for housing, meals, and incidentals. The W2 + Per Diem model is better for everyone — here's why:
Cost ComponentW2 OnlyW2 + Per Diem
W2 Compensation$54.81/hr$20.00/hr
Overhead % (only on W2)25%33%
Overhead in Dollars$13.70$6.60
Per Diem (Non-Taxable)$0.00$34.81
Total Cost to Company$68.51$61.41
Gross Profit/hr$10.96$18.06
Why this works: Overhead (payroll taxes, insurance, workers comp) only applies to the W2 portion. Even though the overhead percentage is higher (33% vs 25%), 33% of $20 is far less than 25% of $54. The stipend portion passes through with zero overhead. The nurse also wins — they take home about $10/hr more because the stipend is never taxed.
Loading Factor (Overhead)
The loading factor represents the real cost of employing someone beyond their W2 wage. It includes payroll taxes (Social Security, Medicare, FUTA, SUTA), workers compensation insurance, general liability insurance, health/dental/vision benefits, and 401K matching.
W2 RateLoading FactorWhat It Means
Below $30/hr1.33 (33% overhead)Every $1 of W2 costs the company $1.33
$30/hr and above1.25 (25% overhead)Every $1 of W2 costs the company $1.25
Which Tab Should I Use?
Standard (Non-CA): Use for all states except California. This is the most common tab. Supports toggling stipend on/off for OT hours and orientation hours. Allows a separate OT stipend rate.
California OT: California has unique overtime laws. Stipend automatically applies to all hours — there is no toggle. The Vivian fee formula differs slightly (includes OT hours in the denominator).
OGS Nurse: For OGS government contracts. Housing and meals are entered as daily amounts. PTO automatically calculated at 4.65% of W2. Fixed 1.25 loading factor. Vivian fee always included.
Tips for New Recruiters
Check the margin first. Above $10/hr is healthy. $5-10/hr is thin but workable. Below $5/hr is risky.
Keep W-2 low. $13-$20/hr is typical. Lower W2 means less overhead and more margin.
Orange fields are required for every deal. Gray fields are pre-filled defaults.
Vivian adds $2-3/hr cost. Direct sourcing saves that amount of margin.
Present Weekly Gross to the nurse. That's the number they care about most.
Use History tab to compare similar past packages and stay consistent.
If margin turns red, the deal loses money on every hour worked. Either reduce the nurse package, negotiate a higher bill rate, or walk away from the deal.
